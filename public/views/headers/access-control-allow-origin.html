"...allowing cross-site HTTP requests from any domain is a bad idea." Tony DeLaGrange, Secure Ideas What You Do Not Want: Access-Control-Allow-Origin: *

Tell Me More

Modern websites often include content dynamically pulled in from other sources online. SoundCloud, Flickr, Youtube and many other important websites use a technique called Cross Object Resource Sharing (CORS) to do so. Access Control Allow Origin is a header that is part of the "conversation" between the site a that wants to include data from another site. If your server includes this in its headers:

Access-Control-Allow-Origin: http://www.example-social-network.com

... it means that example-social-network.com is permitted to integrate your site's data and content. If your server includes this in its headers:

Access-Control-Allow-Origin: *

... it means that anyone is permitted to integrate your site's data and content, and being so blindly "promiscuous" is generally seen as risky because it opens the door to malicious or abusive CORS shenanigans. As others put it:

"This is the most common and obvious mistake that implementers can be make." - Attack and Defense Labs


This is why good parties have invitation lists - you don't want to let any guy off the street crash your party!
