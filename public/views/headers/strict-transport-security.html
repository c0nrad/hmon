Strict-Transport-Security: max-age=31536000; includeSubDomains


Tell Me More

The HTTP Strict Transport Security (HSTS) Header is low hanging fruit when it comes to improving the security of your website. Everyone knows that SSL / TLS encrypts the communication between the browser and the webserver, provides assurance that the data hasn't been tampered with by a third party, and authenticates your organization to users. Why not increase the likelihood that those benefits are extended across your site?

Using the HSTS header tells browsers that they should first make requests to your site over HTTPS by default! Sure, you could use server-side rewrites to redirect requests to HTTPS versions of content, but why spend those computational cycles? As Ilya Grigorik points out in his book High Performance Browser Networking, if you use HSTS the browser will automatically request HTTPS content first so in addition to increasing security you can consider it a performance tweak for your site.

Moreover, you can use the max-age parameter to indicate that the browser should continue to do so for a length of time you wish!

HSTS is a no-brainer if your site already offers HTTPS encryption.
