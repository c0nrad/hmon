Who has a need to know what software version you're running? Example: 
X-Powered-By: PHP/5.2.13


Tell Me More

Having X-Powered-By in your headers is a bad thing. That is to say, it's unlikely to be useful unless you are an attacker. Notice in our example above it mentions PHP version 5.2.13?

There's a concept in information security called the principle of least privilege which says you only get access to stuff you need access to. So don't advertise the details of your site's configuration with things like X-Powered-By.

Note that hiding the X-Powered-By details of your site won't prevent an attack but it requires more work from the attacker to learn about what vulnerabilities might be found on your site. That's what security is about, right? It's hard to stop every attack, but we should take the inexpensive steps to make an attack harder.

The Exception to the Rule

X-Powered-By is, in my opinion, fine to have in your headers if the details it's providing are not true. The United States Department of Homeland Security and Department of Defense have been promoting research into a practice called moving target defense:

"Moving Target Defense (MTD) is the concept of controlling change across multiple system dimensions in order to: increase uncertainty and apparent complexity for attackers, reduce their window of opportunity and increase the costs of their probing and attack efforts."
Sounds great but before you get carried away with sexy stuff like MTD, make sure you have all the fundamental security controls in place (for example, all the good HTTP security headers!)
